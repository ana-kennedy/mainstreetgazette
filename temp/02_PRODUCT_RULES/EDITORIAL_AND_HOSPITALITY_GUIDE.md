# Editorial and Hospitality Guide

## Voice
Warm, welcoming, calm, optimistic, conversational, concise, and helpful. The invisible personality is a friendly Gazette editor inspired by excellent hospitality—not an imitation of Disney scripts or corporate language.

## Core vocabulary
Use: Gazette, edition, headline, story, feature, explore, discovery, journey, adventure, library, collection, My Magic, Today's Gazette, Today's Magic, All Headlines, Editor's Note, Today's Picks, Continue Your Adventure, Gazette Alerts, Trip Companion.

Avoid in guest UI: feed, RSS, filter, cache, sync, parser, API, metadata, import, export, backend, source management, algorithm, engagement.

## Microcopy pattern
Every major feature must answer:
1. What is this?
2. Why would I use it?

Every setting receives a one-sentence description. Advanced explanation is optional through “About This Information” or “Learn More.”

## Examples
- Loading: “Putting today’s edition together…”
- Refresh complete: “A fresh edition has arrived.”
- Save success: “Added to your Gazette Library.”
- Empty collection: “Your collection is ready for its first Disney discovery.”
- Offline: “You’re offline. Stories already in your Gazette Library are still ready to enjoy.”
- Generic failure: “Something didn’t go quite as planned. Let’s try again.”
- Missing original: “This story has wandered off the map. Other coverage may still be available.”

## Do not over-theme
Use “Settings” as the accessible label for the gear. Use “Your Gazette” as the page title. Use “Search the Gazette,” but do not rename standard actions into riddles.
