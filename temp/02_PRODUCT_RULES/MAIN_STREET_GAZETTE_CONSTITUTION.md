# The Main Street Gazette Constitution

1. **Technology stays backstage.** Never expose RSS, API, parser, metadata, database, cache, sync, backend, source list, or ingestion terminology in normal guest-facing UI.
2. **Be familiar before being delightful.** Keep standard iOS icons, gestures, navigation, and permission behavior. Add magic through hospitality, wording, polish, sound, and visual atmosphere.
3. **Every interaction must reduce effort or increase delight.** Otherwise remove it.
4. **Never expose an empty promise.** Hide adaptive sections until enough useful content exists.
5. **The user owns the memories; the app manages the mechanics.** Saved items, collections, trip information, and preferences belong to the user. Storage optimization and backfill remain automatic.
6. **The Gazette grows with its reader.** Search, relationships, destination pages, and recommendations become richer as metadata accumulates.
7. **Never make decisions for the guest.** Offer context and invitations; do not assume interests, create collections, or reorganize personal content without permission.
8. **One preference should influence many experiences.** My Magic powers relevant highlights across News, Explore, For You, and eligible alerts.
9. **Clarity wins over cleverness.** Magical wording must never obscure an action or destination.
10. **Accessibility is structural, not decorative.** VoiceOver, braille, low vision, keyboard, Dynamic Type, Reduce Motion, and contrast behavior are designed into every component.
11. **Trust is visible.** Identify live, recently updated, estimated, planning-guide, editorial, official, and independent information.
12. **Restraint is part of the magic.** Avoid castles, fireworks, Mickey shapes, gradients, sounds, and animations everywhere. Use atmosphere sparingly.
